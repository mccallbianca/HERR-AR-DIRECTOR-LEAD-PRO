The questions you probably already have

Is this a one-time project where I produce tracks and then I'm done?
Not exactly — and this is the important distinction. The catalog is ongoing. You are not being asked to produce a fixed number of tracks and walk away. You are being asked to build the foundation of a living music catalog that grows with the platform. Phase 1 gets us to launch. Phase 2 expands the catalog and brings in a network of producers you will lead and curate. Your tracks keep earning on every play indefinitely — but the creative work continues as the platform grows, on a pace we define together.
Are members going to listen to the same music on repeat forever?
No. New music drops on a consistent weekly release schedule — the same way a streaming platform releases new music every Friday. Members rate tracks, build personal playlists, and discover new releases. The catalog is always growing. What makes each daily listening experience unique is that the subliminal affirmation layer beneath your music is different every morning — AI-generated I AM declarations in each member's own cloned voice, personalized to their current clinical profile. Same music, different inner voice beneath it, every single day.
How does the royalty work — and how do I know I'll actually earn?
The royalty model is a pro-rata stream share — the same model Spotify and Apple Music use. A fixed percentage of Net Revenue is allocated as the Music Royalty Pool. Your earnings equal your tracks' share of total platform streams. The pool is capped — ECQO never pays more than the pool regardless of how many producers are in the catalog. As the Lead Producer setting the standard in Phase 1, your tracks will dominate early streaming. The full math is in the Income section below — with real numbers, no jargon.
How many tracks are we actually talking about for launch?
The beta launch catalog is 24 tracks — 8 genres × 3 priority activity modes (Sleep, Morning, Workout) at one intensity level each. That is a real, launchable catalog that proves the concept and gets members paying from day one. The full vision is 64 tracks — all 8 genres × all 8 activity modes — built in phases as the platform generates revenue. Intensity level variations are a Phase 3 expansion. You do not produce that alone or all at once. And I want to talk about building a small team from the start — more on that in the timeline section.

Layer 3 — Your Creative Brief

What you can do, what you cannot do, and why

This section answers the creative questions directly — based on the science of subliminal audio therapy, binaural beat entrainment research, and therapeutic music design. These are not arbitrary rules. Every guardrail exists because violating it breaks the clinical mechanism.

You CAN include
✓ Wordless vocals — humming, harmonizing, melodic runs without words
✓ Instrumental ad libs — musical fills, riffs, production flourishes
✓ Ambient sound textures — rain, nature sounds, spatial elements that do not demand attention
✓ Atmospheric sound design — pads, reverb tails, textural layers
✓ Gradual dynamic builds — slow, predictable arc from sparse to full and back
✓ Genre-authentic texture — gospel choir breathiness, hip hop vinyl crackle, Latin guitar warmth
You CANNOT include
✕ Lyrics — any conscious language in Layer 3 competes directly with the subliminal affirmations in Layer 1. The brain cannot process two simultaneous linguistic streams. Lyrics will always win. Affirmations lose. The treatment breaks.
✕ Spoken word or vocal chanting with words — same reason
✕ Jarring drops, hard transitions, or sudden sonic surprises — these snap the brain out of alpha/theta/delta state. Brainwave entrainment requires sonic predictability.
✕ Dramatic tempo changes or key modulations — same reason. Consistency is the mechanism.
✕ Third-party samples or licensed elements — creates IP complications for the platform and the Joint IP Agreement

The science behind the no-lyrics rule: Research in subliminal audio therapy (Budzynski 1977, Becker and Sheridan 1990, confirmed in multiple binaural beat studies) consistently demonstrates that conscious linguistic content in the foreground masks subliminal linguistic content. The music layer must be emotionally resonant but cognitively non-demanding — it occupies the emotional brain, not the language brain. Wordless vocals are permitted because they carry emotional resonance without semantic content.

Your creative latitude is real: Within these guardrails, the emotional character, sonic texture, arrangement arc, genre authenticity, and production quality are entirely yours. A Gospel Healing track should feel unmistakably like gospel. A Hip Hop Workout track should feel unmistakably like hip hop. The clinical constraints shape the container. Your artistry fills it.

How You Get Paid — Plain Language

The pro-rata royalty model, explained simply

The royalty model used here is the same model that powers Spotify, Apple Music, and Tidal. It is designed so ECQO never pays more than it can afford regardless of how many producers are in the catalog — and so producers who make better tracks earn more.

How you get paid — in plain English
No jargon. Real numbers. Monthly.

Every month, ECQO sets aside 30% of Net Revenue specifically for producers. That is the Music Royalty Pool — it is ring-fenced before anything else happens. Net Revenue just means what comes in from subscriptions after payment fees and refunds.

Your share of that pool is based on how many plays your tracks received compared to all tracks on the platform that month. If your tracks accounted for half of all plays, you receive half the pool. If they accounted for a quarter, you receive a quarter. Better tracks earn more. More tracks earn more. Both are in your control.

This model is the same one Spotify and Apple Music use — it is the music industry standard for platforms with multiple contributing artists. The difference here is that ECQO pays producers directly, every month, with a transparent statement showing plays per track and exactly how the pool was divided.

Payment is monthly — not quarterly. You see what your tracks earned and receive payment within 15 days of the end of each calendar month.

Why this protects everyone: Because the pool is capped at 30% of revenue, ECQO can never be bankrupted by royalties no matter how many producers join the catalog. Because your earnings are based on your share of streams — not a fixed percentage of all revenue — you benefit directly from producing tracks that members love and play repeatedly. Quality is rewarded. Volume is rewarded. Both are in your control.

ECQO Sound Studio Presents:
A Moment for Music

This is not in the contract. It is not a deliverable. It is an invitation — because if you say yes, it makes the music more powerful, protects both of us more completely, and turns every track release into a content moment that drives people to the platform.

The concept

For each track you produce, record a short — 60 to 90 seconds — of you in the studio making a real production decision. Not a polished video. Not a performance. A genuine moment: choosing a chord voicing, adjusting the 808 tuning, layering the choir, talking through why you made a specific creative choice for that activity mode. Branded as ECQO Sound Studio Presents: A Moment for Music. Distributed on TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and inside HERR Nation — with every post driving people to the app.

Why it protects you

Under current U.S. copyright law, AI-assisted work must demonstrate substantial human creative authorship to be protected. A behind-the-scenes video of you at the DAW making real production decisions is timestamped, visual, and irrefutable documentation of that authorship.

It is better than a session file. Better than a signed statement. Better than any written record. It is a documented creative act — showing exactly what you decided, when, and why. If anyone ever challenges the copyright on a track, that video is your evidence.

Every short you post is a timestamped copyright record that lives on the public internet forever.

Why it grows the platform

"How I made this beat" content is one of the highest-performing categories on TikTok and Instagram Reels. Producers with millions of followers built their entire audience on exactly this format.

ECQO Sound has a story no other producer content has — the science layer, the clinical purpose, the subliminal technology beneath the music. That is not just a beat-making video. That is a documentary moment. Every short drives curiosity. Curiosity drives downloads.

HERR Nation members who see the person behind their daily affirmation track feel a human connection that keeps them subscribed.
